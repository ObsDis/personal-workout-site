How much protein do you actually need?

Protein is the macro people overthink the most. The short version: most people training for muscle do well somewhere around 0.7 to 1 gram of protein per pound of body weight per day. For a 180 pound lifter that is roughly 125 to 180 grams. More than that rarely hurts, but it also rarely helps once you are in that range.

Why the range works

Protein gives your body the raw material to repair and build muscle after training, and it is the most filling of the three macros, which helps with appetite whether you are cutting or bulking. The exact number inside the range matters less than hitting it consistently, day after day.

Cutting versus bulking

When you are in a calorie deficit, lean toward the higher end. Higher protein protects muscle while you lose fat and keeps you fuller on fewer calories. When bulking, the lower end is usually plenty, since you have energy to spare and carbs and fat have more room.

How to actually hit it

  • Anchor every meal with a protein source: eggs, chicken, fish, lean beef, Greek yogurt, tofu, or a shake.
  • Aim for a rough target per meal. Three or four meals at 35 to 50 grams gets most people there.
  • Track for a week to calibrate your eye. After that you will not need to log every bite.
